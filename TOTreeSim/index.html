About

A sim about patience, care, and something that grows whether you're watching or not.

It Grows While You Live Your Life

TOTreeSim starts with a seed. You plant it, you water it, you pull the weeds that want to crowd it out. Early on there's not much to do ‚Äî you check in, you tend, you wait. That's intentional. Growth takes time, and rushing it doesn't help.

The clock doesn't pause when you close the game. One real day passes as four game days ‚Äî your tree is growing (or struggling) whether you're watching. Come back after a long weekend in a drought year and you'll notice. Come back every morning to water and you'll notice that too.

The longer you care for it, the more it gives back. A well-tended tree drops better materials, more often. A neglected one still grows ‚Äî just slower, leaner, less generous.

The Tamagotchi of Trees

TOTreeSim has the same emotional hook as a Tamagotchi ‚Äî the attachment you form with something you've kept alive. But it's slower, quieter, and the stakes are gentler. Nothing dies dramatically. It just needs more time to recover than it would have if you'd remembered to water it.

Players who grew up with Tamagotchis will recognise the feeling immediately. Everyone else will develop it.

Your Tree

Pine or Oak ‚Äî different growth, different gifts.

Pine

Grows faster than oak, reaches height sooner, drops resin and cones early. Better in cold. Less structural timber at harvest ‚Äî tall and slender rather than thick and gnarled. Pine players tend to be optimisers. They appreciate efficiency. They also tend to regret harvesting because pines photograph well.

Oak

Slow. Patient. Demands more water in summer, more mulching in autumn. Takes longer to reach the stages where it drops useful materials ‚Äî but when it does, the drops are better. Structural timber from a mature oak is the best building material in the sim. Oak players are committed. They also tend to never actually harvest.

What the Tree Drops

As your tree matures it begins dropping materials ‚Äî seeds, cones, resin, fallen branches, bark strips, leaves. Care quality affects the quality of drops: a well-fed, properly irrigated tree drops cleaner materials more reliably. These drops are used for camp builds and pattern crafting. The better the tree, the better the supplies ‚Äî and the more comfortable your camp becomes.

The Harvest Decision

Later in the sim, when your tree is mature and established, you can choose to harvest part of it ‚Äî a limb, a section of trunk ‚Äî for structural building parts. The tree survives. It continues to grow. But it's changed, and you made that choice. Some players can't bring themselves to do it. Others find it the most satisfying moment in the whole sim. There's no right answer, which is exactly the point.

The Path

The way out ‚Äî and the reason to come back.

Leaving Camp

The path runs in and out of the 1km map ‚Äî it's the one permeable edge, the one direction you can go that isn't your tree's. Along the path and in the surrounding terrain you find pattern pieces: fragments of crafting blueprints that are sent to you randomly, arriving a piece at a time.

You don't always know what pattern you're assembling until the pieces start to fit. Finding the third piece of a camp decoration blueprint when you thought you were picking up loose materials is one of those small, satisfying moments that accumulates into something meaningful.

Supplies & Patterns

Pattern pieces arrive on a randomised schedule ‚Äî the game sends them, you find them. This keeps path exploration purposeful without making it compulsory. You come back to camp, you tend the tree, you head out when the day calls for it.

Supplies found on the path complement what the tree drops, letting you build out your camp and make it comfortable. The more you build, the more at-home the environment feels ‚Äî and the tree notices.

Features

Everything that makes this more than a waiting game.

Time Compression

One real day equals four game days. The tree grows on its schedule, not yours. Miss a week in a summer heatwave and the difference is visible. Stay consistent and the tree repays you.

Care Systems

Water, feed, weed, mulch, irrigate, control temperature. Each affects growth rate and drop quality differently. More options unlock as the tree matures ‚Äî early on, watering is enough. Later, it appreciates more.

Camp Building

Build up the camp around the tree using drops and path supplies. The more comfortable and complete the camp, the more the tree responds ‚Äî better drops, faster recovery from stress. The tree likes having a home too.

The Drone

Unlock a drone to fly around your tree and record footage. Pure emotional payoff ‚Äî no gameplay function, just your tree from above, in motion, in all seasons. This feature ends up in every screenshot. We know this.

Seasons & Weather

Your tree experiences everything. Drought, frost, storms, ideal spring rain. Weather is not always gentle. Knowing the forecast and responding appropriately is part of the care loop.

Pattern Crafting

Randomly delivered blueprint fragments, found on the path. Assemble them over time into camp decorations and useful objects. The randomness keeps it interesting ‚Äî you never quite know what's coming next.
