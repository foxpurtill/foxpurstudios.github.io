About

A meadow, a stream, a warehouse, and time.

Your Meadow. Your Pace.

Every new game generates a fresh meadow — never the same twice. Somewhere on the map there's always a stream. At the bottom of the map, always, is the warehouse: the one fixed point, the place where everything important gets stored. You can't leave the 2km boundary. You don't need to. Everything you need to build your dream hut is out there, somewhere, waiting to be found.

You search the meadow for parts. Parts match blueprints — but blueprints arrive in pieces too, so you might find the components for a chimney before you know you're building one. Some parts don't match anything. Some look identical to parts that do (that long tool handle could be a hoe or a rake — you won't know until you pick it up).

Easy Play

There's no combat. No experience points. No leaderboard. Seasons change, days pass, weather rolls in. A small fire might damage your build. A flood might rearrange your plans. Animals wander through.

Nothing is permanent damage. Everything can be repaired. The meadow isn't trying to beat you — it's just being a meadow.

Save Your Meadow, Start a New One

When you're done with a game you can save your meadow and return to it. Or start fresh — new meadow, new blueprint hunt, new hut to imagine. Old saves stay warm and waiting. Nothing gets deleted unless you choose it.

How It Works

The loop, explained gently.

1. Search

Explore the meadow to find parts scattered across the landscape. Some are obvious. Some are hidden. Some are in places the weather put them. Parts go to the warehouse until needed.

2. Match

Blueprint pieces arrive — randomly, at their own pace. Match parts to blueprints to unlock building stages. Wrong parts can be sold; right parts can be sourced from the trader if you've sold them by accident (it happens to everyone).

3. Build

Follow the blueprint. Your hut takes shape piece by piece. Seasons change around it. Weather tests it. A small fire or a summer flood might mean a morning of repairs — but the build is yours, and it persists.

The Warehouse

The one constant in every meadow. Everything you collect goes here. It doesn't flood, it doesn't burn, it doesn't move. Think of it as your anchor — the reason you can wander far and still come home.

The Trader

A visiting trader lets you sell surplus parts for coin, and spend coin on parts you need — or accidentally sold. Not everything is always in stock. Patience helps, as it does with most things in the meadow.

Features

Everything that makes the meadow feel like yours.

Procedural Meadow

Every new game is a fresh 2km world. Different landscape, different part distribution, different feel. Save and return to favourites, or let them go and start again.

Blueprint Discovery

Blueprints arrive in fragments. You might not know what you're building until the pieces start to fit. The moment of recognition is its own reward.

Ambiguous Parts

That long handle — is it a hoe or a rake? Parts that look alike can be different things. The meadow keeps you honest and occasionally keeps you guessing.

Seasons & Weather

Spring rains, summer heat, autumn winds, winter frost. Day and night cycle. Temperature affects comfort. Weather affects the meadow. It all feels like somewhere real.

Damage & Repair

Fire, lightning, flood, and animals can damage your build. Nothing is catastrophic. Repairs are part of ownership — and there's something satisfying about fixing something you made.

The Stream

Every meadow has one. It's always nearby, always moving. Not a game mechanic — just the meadow being a meadow. Sit by it. It helps.
